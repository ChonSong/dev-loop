## Key Insight

Three forces shaping Sydney's desk-job market in 2026:
1. **AI disruption** — hitting white-collar first. Roles with routine data/doc work are shrinking. Roles requiring judgment, relationships, and cross-functional coordination are growing.
2. **Public sector retreat** — government office roles shrinking (-1.0%). Not absorbing volume.
3. **Population growth outpacing job creation** — 1.9% vs 1.2%. More applicants per role.

## Best Entry-Level Pathways

1. Temp agency registration (Hays, Randstad) — $30-45/hr, foot in the door
2. Graduate programs (Accenture, CBA, Deloitte, EY, BHP, NSW Gov)
3. Direct-entry employers (IAG, QBE, Allianz, CBA, Westpac, NSW Gov)
4. Non-degree route: TAFE traineeship or NFP sector

## AI Risk by Role

**HIGH (shrinking, 0.5% growth/yr):** Data entry, bookkeeper, paralegal (doc review), insurance underwriter, copywriter (basic), customer service (non-complex)

**MODERATE (changing shape):** Accountant (shift to advisory), software dev (shift to architecture), legal (shift to negotiation), marketing (shift to strategy)

**LOW (stable/growing):** EA, HRBP, procurement, account/client management, sales, risk/compliance, project management, change management, recruitment
