## The Work Environment — Full Syllabus Content

**NESA Syllabus Reference:** Core 3 — The Economic Environment (Term 3)
**Indicative hours:** 25 hours (8–10 weeks)

### Syllabus Content Points
- **Work and wellbeing**: The meaning and value of work; factors affecting work choices
- **The workplace**: Forms of employment (full-time, part-time, casual, contract); employment relationships; rights and responsibilities
- **Rights and responsibilities**: National Employment Standards (NES); Fair Work Act 2009; Workplace Health and Safety; discrimination law
- **Current issues**: Gig economy; automation; changing work patterns

### Skills
- Thinking, researching, and communicating
- Evaluating employment contracts
- Researching career pathways

### Outcomes
**COM:** CO5-RRI-01, CO5-DEC-01, CO5-PRO-01, CO5-COM-01, CO5-ECB-01, CO5-WOR-01
**Life Skills:** COLS-RRI-01, COLS-DEC-01, COLS-PRO-01, COLS-COM-01, COLS-WOR-01

## Glossary of Key Terms — The Work Environment

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Award | Document outlining an employee's minimum pay and conditions |
| Cadetship | Training in a full-time job, with time allocated for TAFE/university |
| Casual | Employed on an 'as needed' basis; receives loading instead of entitlements |
| Common law contract | Individually negotiated contract between employer and employee |
| Discrimination | Treating a person less favourably due to protected attributes (gender, ethnicity, religion, disability) |
| Employee | Person who works for a wage or salary |
| Employer | Person who hires employees |
| Employment contract | Legally binding formal agreement between employee and employer |
| Enterprise agreement | Agreement about pay and conditions made at the enterprise/workplace level |
| Entitlements | Non-wage benefits such as long service leave, sick leave, paid annual leave |
| Full-time | Continuing, ongoing employment working 38 hours or more per week |
| Loading | Extra pay to compensate casual employees for lack of access to entitlements |
| Part-time | Ongoing employment working fewer than 38 hours per week |
| Labour force | People aged 15 and over who are employed or unemployed |
| Gig economy | Short-term, task-based work through digital platforms (Uber, Airtasker, Deliveroo) |
| Superannuation | 11% of ordinary time earnings (2025–26 rate); must be paid to a nominated fund |
| NES | National Employment Standards — 10 minimum entitlements for all employees |
| Fair Work Act | Federal legislation governing workplace relations in Australia |
| WHS | Work Health and Safety — duty of care obligations for employers and employees |

## Assignment 1 Task Descriptor (reusable in UbD Unit)

**Task name:** Workplace Dispute Resolution Report
**Subject:** Commerce Year 10, Stage 3 Assessment Task 1
**Role:** Fair Work Inspector

**Scenario — Peter the Part-Time Worker:**
Peter is a 16-year-old who works part-time at a restaurant. Issues: $12/hr flat rate (no weekend loading), no payslips, no superannuation, denied meal breaks on weekends, sent home early or required to stay late unpaid, manager becomes aggressive when questioned.

**Required components:**
1. Explain Peter's legal rights and the employer's responsibilities
2. Analyse if there is workplace grievance and the impact on both parties
3. Recommend a strategy to resolve the issue professionally

**NESA verbs assessed:** Explain, Analyse, Recommend
**Outcomes assessed:** COM5-1, COM5-2, COM5-6, COM5-8 (旧代码) / CO5-COM-01, CO5-RRI-01, CO5-DEC-01, CO5-PRO-01 (新代码)
**Due:** Term 3, Week 7 | **Weighting:** 25%
