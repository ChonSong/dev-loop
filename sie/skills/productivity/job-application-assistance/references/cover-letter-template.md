# Cover Letter Template for Graduate Programs

## Structure

| Section | Word Count | Purpose |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Opening | 2–3 sentences | Name the specific role + company; express genuine interest |
| Why this company | 2–3 sentences | Reference specific work they do (industries, services, clients) |
| Why this track | 2–3 sentences | Connect your background to 2 specific tracks/pathways |
| Concrete example | 3–4 sentences | One project/internship/thesis: what, how, what outcome |
| Values/culture | 1–2 sentences | Echo company's stated culture and diversity commitments |
| Close | 1–2 sentences | Invite discussion; thank the reader |

**Total: 300–400 words**

---

## Opening Formula

> "I am writing to express my strong interest in the [YEAR] [COMPANY] [PROGRAM NAME], specifically applying for the [TRACK] pathway."

Avoid: "I am a highly motivated individual looking for a challenging opportunity." — This is filler and tells the recruiter nothing.

---

## Why This Company Formula

Name specific things the company does:
- Clients they serve (financial services, healthcare, government, energy)
- Services they provide (digital transformation, managed services, cloud migration, consulting)
- Scale or reputation (global, Australian market leader, Fortune 500)

Avoid generic praise ("innovative leader in the industry"). Name what they actually do.

---

## Why This Track Formula

> "I am particularly drawn to [TRACK A] because it aligns with my [specific skill/experience], and to [TRACK B] because..."

Don't just list tracks. Name one concrete reason each connects to your background.

---

## Concrete Example Formula

> "In my [capstone project / internship / part-time role] at [organisation], I [action] using [tools/methods] to achieve [specific outcome]. This reinforced my ability to [transferable skill] — a skill central to [COMPANY's] [specific practice]."

**Bad:** "I worked on a project and learned a lot."
**Good:** "I developed a cloud-based inventory management system using Python and AWS for a logistics startup, working directly with the client stakeholder to define requirements and deliver a working solution within a 6-week sprint."

---

## Diversity Statement Formula

Only include if the company explicitly mentions diversity commitments. Match their language:
- DXC: "DXC's commitment to building diverse talent — including its encouragement of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander and neurodiverse applicants — reflects values I share."
- Others: adjust to match their specific phrasing (Indigenous, accessibility, neurodiversity, LGBTQ+, etc.)

---

## Placeholder Checklist

Mark every `[BRACKETED ITEM]` so it is clear what needs personalisation:
- [TRACK] — the specific pathway
- [SPECIFIC CLIENT/INDUSTRY] — name 1–2 actual industries DXC works in
- [DEGREE TYPE] — e.g., Computer Science, Software Engineering
- [COMPLETION DATE] — month and year
- [PROJECT/INTERNSHIP/THESIS] — specific example
- [CONCRETE EXAMPLE] — what you did, tools used, outcome
- [YOUR NAME], [PHONE], [LINKEDIN]

---

## Common Mistakes

1. **Generic opening** — "I am excited to apply for this position." Name the role and company every time.
2. **Listing skills without evidence** — "I have strong problem-solving skills" means nothing without an example.
3. **Cover letter is a repeat of resume** — the cover letter should explain *why this company and track*, not re-list your experience.
4. **Too long** — >400 words signals you cannot be concise. Recruiters read hundreds of these.
5. **Wrong company name** — always verify the hiring company. DXC is not the same as HPE Enterprise Services (its predecessor).
6. **Missing eligibility** — Australian graduate programs often require citizenship or permanent residency. If you mention it, get the requirement right.
