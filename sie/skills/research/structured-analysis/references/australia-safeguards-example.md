# Worked Example: Australia's AGI Safeguards

From BlueDot's "Future of AI" course exercise: *"Imagine your country built AGI and most of the population become economically irrelevant. What existing safeguards would stop your politicians becoming dictators, and how robust are these?"*

## Components Evaluated

| Safeguard | Robustness | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Constitution (Separation of powers, High Court) | 🟡 Moderate | Written constitution, judicial review, but NO bill of rights — rights are implied or statutory, not entrenched. Section 61 (executive power) is elastic. |
| Federalism (State powers) | 🟡 Moderate | States retain police, health, education. Commonwealth controls tax and defence — financial strangulation is the historical playbook for centralisation. Weakens over time. |
| Westminster conventions (Responsible government) | 🔴 Weak | PM answers to Parliament, Parliament to people. But party discipline means executive already dominates. If population is economically irrelevant, what's the electoral threat? Ritual elections. |
| Public service (APS, AAT, Ombudsman, Auditor-General) | 🟢 High → 🔴 None | Strong merit-based norms now. But AGI can replace 150,000 bureaucrats — the institutional memory and quiet resistance disappear the moment the government automates the APS. |
| Media / civil society (Concentration, ABC) | 🔴 Weak | Already high media concentration (Murdoch). ABC independent by charter but funding-dependent. AGI-powered personalised disinformation drowns the information environment without needing censorship. |
| Governor-General (Reserve powers) | 🔴 Paper tiger | Can dismiss PM, refuse dissolution. Used in 1975. But G-G is PM-appointed — in a dictatorship scenario they'd appoint a loyalist. |

## Verdict

Australia's safeguards are designed for 20th-century threats — a PM who misleads parliament, a minister who rorts expenses, slow norm erosion over decades. Not for a government that doesn't need its citizens for anything and controls the most powerful influence technology ever built.

The scariest path isn't a coup — it's a legally elected government that slowly, over two or three terms, makes itself impossible to remove while maintaining all the *forms* of democracy.

## What Made This Work

1. **The frame was clear** — restated the course exercise in the user's context (Australia)
2. **Decomposition was natural** — the prompt implied a comparison of different safeguard types; each became a sub-section
3. **Each component got a specific rating** — not just "weak" but *why* and *under what conditions*
4. **AGI-specific vulnerability** was identified per component (e.g. "AGI can replace the APS", "AGI-powered disinformation")
5. **The summary was honest** — not Pollyanna-ish about democracy, not doom-mongering either
